They Say, We Say

This piece was created for the OSSFT contest in 2023. Please be aware that button leads outside the site.


Written Poetry


I've played peasant, bard, queen and king,
I've played up and down and everything,
    I’ll talk and laugh and choke and sing.
I've written rules, then broke the rules
Because I've played but one thing, and it's The Fool.

You think these masks are part of me?
I won't deny that that could be,
In this world of inconsistency.
They could be me, or they could be tools,
After all, am I not The Fool?

Word of advice, when they wonder what’s true,
You only say "I'm one of You"
    Till truth is lies and lies are true.
    Promise on a spent life, it's the thing to do.
Play it up and play it cool,
To survive you are The Fool.

Just be sure to be the jester with gesture who plays just sure enough,
That they believe all your lies, and think all your truths are bluff.
And beneath the teeth lined smiles and saccharine words
The lies you tell can not be heard.

Hold a smile in shaking hands,
On what unstable ground do traitors stand.
But what road is there to survival but to betray
All belief that there was a better way.

They say I have luck and lucky chance
But have they played this deathly dance?
Of truth and lies and broken rules
Where in the end, I'm played the fool.

And if you actually read of that, Penner offers her condolensces to your sanity, as well as her best stock of eye bleach so you might have a chance of recovery.
