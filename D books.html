Books


The Will of the Many

For her HSP3U ISU, Penner created a psychological analysis of a character, from The Will of the Many, written by James Islington.


The Licanius Trilogy

The Licanius Trilogy, by James Islington, is a high fantasy novel that follows its four main characters in a battle against a great evil coming upon their land. Despite appearing cliche at first, this novel developes themes within the ideas of responsibility, justification and true strength.


Wings of Fire

Even though its a middlegrade series, Wings of Fire by Tui T. Sutherlandbuilds a detailed world that establishes ideas of friendship, equality and happiness. Following the stories of the dragons within its world as they fight to bring peace to a war-orn world, it discusses the complex emotions within each individual which contribute to how they function as part of society.


Lord of the Rings

Lord of the Rings, one of the most famous high fantasy novels, is an epic book and movie for its strong plot and powerful portrayal of characters. The stories of Sam, Frodo, Aragorn and the rest all able resonate with the reader through Tolkien's use of description and structure of plot. There is a good reason they are still popular after 70 years.
